Ojude Oba 2017

The Ojude Oba Festival, one of Nigeria’s most iconic cultural events, took center stage in Ijebu-Ode in 2017, showcasing the rich traditions and heritage of the Ijebu people. Held annually on the third day after Eid-el-Kabir, this festival gathers indigenes, visitors, and dignitaries from all walks of life to celebrate unity, culture, and community spirit under the esteemed patronage of the Awujale of Ijebuland, Oba Sikiru Kayode Adetona. The 2017 festival was themed around unity and progress, highlighting the importance of cultural preservation in a rapidly modernizing world. The event featured the famous parade of Regberegbes (age-grade groups), equestrian displays, musical performances, and a showcase of traditional attire and craftsmanship.

One of the standout participants of Ojude Oba 2017 was the cultural society Bobamayegun Okunrin Akile Ijebu. This distinguished group captured the hearts of spectators with their exceptional and radiant display of Ijebu culture. Dressed in vibrant, elaborately embroidered traditional attire, their synchronized performances and chants reverberated through the festival grounds, embodying the essence of pride and cultural identity. Their procession was a perfect blend of historical reverence and contemporary flair. The Bobamayegun Okunrin Akile Ijebu also paid homage to the Awujale with gifts and tributes, reflecting their commitment to the values of respect and communal harmony that the festival promotes. Their presentation was both a spectacle and a reminder of the enduring legacy of Ijebu customs, earning them widespread admiration from attendees and praise in local media.
