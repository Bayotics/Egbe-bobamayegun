Ileya party at Conference Hotel 2018

The Bobamayegun Okunrin Akile Ijebu society fondly remembers the remarkable Ileya celebration held at the prestigious Conference Hotel in 2018. This event was a true reflection of our cultural heritage, unity, and commitment to sustaining the vibrant Yoruba traditions. The Ileya festival, marking the Islamic Eid-el-Kabir, has always been a significant celebration among the Yoruba people. The 2018 edition was no different, bringing together Ijebu indigenes from all walks of life to rejoice in a grand setting. The Conference Hotel in Ogun State provided the perfect backdrop for a day filled with warmth, joy, and cultural pride. Guests at the event were dressed in stunning Yoruba traditional attire, showcasing the beauty and elegance of our culture. Flowing agbada, intricately designed aso-oke, and radiant iro and buba added a kaleidoscope of colors to the occasion.

No Ileya celebration is complete without a feast, and the 2018 party offered a sumptuous spread of traditional dishes. Guests enjoyed tender, flavorful ram meat, a staple of the Ileya feast, alongside beloved delicacies such as jollof rice, amala, ewedu, and iyan with assorted soups. Refreshments ranged from palm wine and zobo to fine imported wines, catering to every taste. The event also honored notable members and distinguished guests for their contributions to the Ijebu community. These recognitions underscored the importance of community service and the spirit of giving back, which are key values of the Bobamayegun Okunrin Akile Ijebu society. The Ileya Party at Conference Hotel in 2018 was not just a celebration; it was a unifying experience that connected generations. It reinforced our shared identity and inspired the younger generation to take pride in our traditions. Events like this remain a source of inspiration and a reminder of the richness of our heritage.
