Annual General Meeting of Bobamayegun Okunrin Akile Ijebu (March 2024, Texas)

The Bobamayegun Okunrin Akile Ijebu cultural society hosted its highly anticipated Annual General Meeting (AGM), fondly referred to as "Ojude," in March 2024. The event was held in Texas and brought together members of the vibrant Ijebu cultural community to celebrate heritage, review organizational progress, and chart the course for future activities.

The meeting opened with a traditional procession led by prominent elders and dignitaries of the society, accompanied by cultural music and drumming that evoked the rich heritage of Ijebu land. Members adorned in traditional attires, such as agbadas and fila caps for men, showcased the elegance and pride associated with their identity. During the AGM, the president of the society, Chief Adeyemi Olowu, delivered a keynote address reflecting on the society’s achievements in the past year. These included outreach programs, educational sponsorships, and cultural events aimed at promoting the Ijebu identity among younger generations in the diaspora.

The meeting concluded with a grand feast, where attendees enjoyed Ijebu delicacies like ikokore, efo riro, and asun. It was an occasion marked by unity, pride, and a shared commitment to preserving Ijebu heritage. As the society moves forward under its newly elected leadership, members left the AGM with renewed enthusiasm to strengthen their community and promote the values of Bobamayegun Okunrin Akile Ijebu for future generations.
